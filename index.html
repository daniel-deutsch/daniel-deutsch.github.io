Studies

[2022] Effects of Social Media Bots on the Crypto Market

The study scraped 6 million social media posts related to the top 50 cryptocurrencies by market capitalization. Then, applied a framework that leverages a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) and the kNN algorithm to classify users as bots or humans. A deep neural network was built to classify the sentiment of each post as either "Bearish" or "Bullish." Finally, generated a signal for each crypto asset based on the engagement rate of their posts and analyzed the signal's effect on the asset's price fluctuations. The study highlights the potential of using machine learning techniques to analyze social media data for insights into cryptocurrency market trends.

Deep Learning Statistical Analysis Big Data Natural Language Processing (NLP) Sentiment Analysis Unsupervised Learning Variational Autoencoder BERT

[2021] How User Information Drives Amazon's Products Recommendations

Scraped information about more than 60,000 Amazon products by mimicking the behavior/interests of seven different personas (sports, shopping, science, home, health, games, and computers) through the usage of cookies and HTTP request parameters. Proceed, then, to analyze whether there is evidence of price discrimination or price steering between each persona's recommended products. Later on, analyzed the importance of each seller's features on Amazon's seller recommendation on the product's buy-box.

Data Scraping Cookies Manipulation Statistical Analysis

[2021] Effects of the Interest Rate on the Brazilian Stock Market

The impacts of the interest rate on eight distinct sectors of the Brazilian stock market were examined using financial indices as proxies for each sector. The research was conducted by applying both a time series (SARIMAX) and a linear regression model.

Time Series Analysis

[2021] Analysis of Economic Incentives of Yelp's Fake Reviews for Parisian Restaurants

Scraped information about more than 250,000 reviews from over 13,000 parisian restaurants on the Yelp social platform and proceeded to analyse the effects of restaurant competitiveness on review fraud, both for self improvement and for harming competitors. Later, the polarization difference between fake and genuine reviews, as well as the characteristics of users who publish fraudulent reviews, were examined.

Data Scraping Natural Language Processing (NLP) Sentiment Analysis Regression Analysis
